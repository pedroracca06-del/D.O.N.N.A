# Candle Closure Rules — Definition and Application

**Source:** `TRANSCRIPTS_RAW/orb_rp_002.txt`
**Extracted:** 2026-05-29

---

## Critical Definition

**A candle closure is NOT a wick touch.**

> "This is not a candle closure. Candle closure means the entire candle is over a level. This would be a candle closure. So with this, this is not a candle closure. This is a rejection."

| Event | Definition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Wick touch** | Candle body stays on one side of the level; only the wick crosses | Rejection — the level is holding |
| **Candle closure** | The entire candle (body + close) closes on the other side of the level | Level is violated — setup interpretation changes |

A wick that crosses a key level and then pulls back is **confirmation that the level is defending** — it is not a breach.

---

## How Candle Closure Is Used Per Setup

### Break and Retest — Confirmation of Direction

On the retest of the ORB midpoint, candle closures confirm that the break direction is holding:

- **Looking for SHORTS**: want to see candle closures **below** the midpoint
- **Looking for LONGS**: want to see candle closures **above** the midpoint

> "I want to see candle closures below the midpoint. Just like this, we tap the midpoint and then we continue to close below it. If price is failing to close above the level in any meaningful way, that's my confirmation."

Entry is taken **on the next candle** after closure confirmation is seen.

### Bounce (Untested Low) — Rejection Candle

- A wick **below** the level with the candle body closing **above** the level = rejection = buyers defended
- A full candle closure **below** the level = level is violated = exit or do not enter

> "A candle with a long lower wick that touches the level, but does not close below it. That wick tells me that sellers pushed the price below that level, but they were not able to keep it below that level because buyers stepped in and rejected it."

### Rejection (Untested High) — Rejection Candle

- A wick **above** the level with the candle body closing **below** the level = rejection = sellers defended
- A full candle closure **above** the level = level is violated

> "Price hit this level and then failed to close above it."

## Multi-Timeframe Closure Hierarchy

Candle closures are assessed across timeframes — higher timeframes carry more weight:

| Timeframe | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 15-minute | Highest | Structural confirmation |
| 5-minute | Medium | Primary execution confirmation |
| 1-minute | Lower | Entry timing; one 1m closure alone insufficient for rejection (shorts example: "only one candle closure above it on the 1m but on the 5m and 15m we didn't have any — meaning we can go ahead and enter") |

> "Key thing here is that we only had one candle closure above it on the 1 minute time frame. But on the 5 minute and on the 15th, we didn't have any candle closures. Meaning we can go ahead and enter some shorts."

**Rule derived:** A single 1m candle closure above a resistance level does not invalidate a rejection short if 5m and 15m show no closures above. The 5m and 15m are the decisive timeframes for confirming whether a level is holding.
