# Level Strength Assessment

**Source:** `TRANSCRIPTS_RAW/orb_rp_002.txt`
**Extracted:** 2026-05-29

---

## Core Principle

Not all levels are equal. The two variables that determine level strength are:

1. **Whether it has been tested** — untested levels are significantly more powerful
2. **How long ago it was formed** — older untested levels are stronger than recent ones

---

## Age of Level = Strength

> "The further back a level is, the stronger it is. If an untested low is from 3 days ago, the trader sitting those positions have been holding for 3 days. They are far more desperate to exit than somebody who's only been in a position for 4 hours. More desperation means more orders, meaning a stronger reaction when price hits that level."

| Level age | Relative strength | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Same session (hours old) | Baseline | Trapped traders have short pain threshold |
| Previous day | Stronger | Positions held overnight |
| 2-3 days old | Strongest | Significantly more desperation to exit at break even |
| Weekly high/low | Extra-strong | Longest hold time, widest trapped position pool |

> "This untested low is also the weekly low, meaning it's going to be a very strong level."

**DISCRETIONARY:** RP does not define an exact age threshold below which a level becomes "too weak." The relative ordering above is stated; the cutoff is judgment-based.

---

## Signs a Level Is Strong

When price approaches a key level, watch the reaction:

| Observation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Wick touches level, candle body closes away | Buyers/sellers defending — level is strong |
| Multiple retests (2+) without candle closure through | Level is accumulating confirmation — strong |
| Strong directional candle immediately off the level | High participation at that price — strong |
| Low volume approach to the level | Fewer participants testing the level — potentially weaker response |

> "We hit this level and then we immediately started showing signs of strength by bouncing off of it once and then bouncing off of it again."

---

## Signs a Level Is Weak — Delete It

> "If price comes to a level and blows straight through it without any reaction, no hesitation, no wick, no sign of respect, that level is weak. Delete it and look for the next untested low."

> "If price shows you zero respect for a level, it's telling you something. Listen to it."

| Observation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Price moves straight through with no wick, no pause | Level is weak — liquidity was already consumed or thin |
| No rejection candle on 5m or 1m | Buyers/sellers not participating at this level |
| Level breaks without any slowing of momentum | Not a real liquidity pool |

**When a level fails:** Remove it from the chart. Do not try to re-enter based on the same level after it has been violated with conviction. Look for the next untested level.

---

## Multi-Timeframe Confluence = Stronger Level

When a level aligns across multiple timeframes (e.g., daily, weekly, session), the liquidity pool is larger:

> "This untested low is also the weekly low, meaning it's going to be a very strong level."

Practical assessment: if a session low is also a weekly low, the number of trapped traders from multiple timeframes is compounded. Reaction is expected to be stronger and more reliable.

**DISCRETIONARY:** RP does not define a minimum confluence count. The weekly low example is one instance of multi-TF confluence. Judgment is required to assess when levels stack meaningfully.

---

## Volume Confirmation at the Level

Volume is used as a secondary filter to confirm whether a level has real institutional activity:

> "When volume is high, it tells you that the levels matter."

Specifically for the 8:00–8:15 candle, RP states this window has the highest volume of any 15-minute period during the day — which is the primary reason it is used as the ORB range. This principle extends to level assessment in general: high-volume reactions at a level signal institutional participation and strengthen the setup.
