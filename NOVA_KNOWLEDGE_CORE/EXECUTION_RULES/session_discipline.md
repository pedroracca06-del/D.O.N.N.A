**Extracted:** 2026-05-30

## Core Principles

**We don't trade because we have to. We trade because we can.**

If no clean setup exists, the correct action is to do nothing. Forcing a trade when the conditions aren't there is not neutral — it is actively worse than not trading. A forced trade is more likely to lose, and it introduces risk that the system never sanctioned.

The market will always be there tomorrow. A missed setup costs nothing. A forced trade can cost real money and real discipline.

The strategy is not the edge. Execution discipline is the edge.

> "I guarantee you 99% of strategies on the market work because everyone's trading the same market. Everyone has a different strategy. It doesn't matter what your strategy is."

The differentiator between profitable and unprofitable traders with the same strategy is whether they can get off the charts and not come back.

---

## Risk Rules

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Risk per trade (Trade 1) | 1% of account |
| Risk per trade (Trade 2) | 0.5% of account — half of Trade 1 |
| Max daily loss | 2% of account — session ends immediately |
| Trade 2 condition | Only permitted after Trade 1 was a **win** |

**Trade 2 after a loss = not permitted.** A loss or break-even on Trade 1 ends the session. The second trade is a reduced-size opportunity earned by a winning first trade, not a right.

Position size is derived from the risk % and the defined stop distance. It is not a fixed contract count.

---

## Trade Limit Per Session

- **Maximum: 2 trades per day**
- Trade 1: full risk (1%)
- Trade 2: only after a win on Trade 1, at half risk (0.5%), same setup quality required
- If no second setup meeting full criteria appears, the session ends after Trade 1

> "You go on the charts with a profitable system. You take one trade a day, maybe two. If you see two good setups, make sure those setups are a part of your plan."

---

## Exit After Each Trade — No Exceptions

After any trade outcome (win / loss / break even): **get off the charts**.

> "If you lose, get off the charts. If you win, get off the charts. If you get break even, get off the charts. Lock yourself out of your accounts."

This applies regardless of P&L. The outcome of one trade does not change whether there is a valid second setup.

---

## Lockout as a Behavioral Tool

When discipline is not yet internalized, use account lockout features as a mechanical guardrail:

> "After I took my first trade, I hit the little button on top that says lock out for the whole day. And then I got used to it. Whether I lost or I won the trade, it doesn't matter. I click the lock out button and I stop."

The purpose is to make it physically impossible to overtrade until the habit is built. Over time, the lockout becomes unnecessary because the behavior is internalized.

---

## Do Not Check the Chart After Exiting

> "Don't go back on your phone later in the day and look at the Trading View chart. Just get off and wait. Wait till tomorrow."

Checking the chart after a trade is closed re-engages the emotional cycle. It serves no strategic purpose and increases the risk of re-entering.

---

## No Dollar Targets Per Session

Setting a specific P&L target for a session forces trades that don't exist:

> "If you're looking at the chart and you're saying, 'Okay, I want to make 10K today.' You're looking at a chart and there's no clean setups. What are you going to do? You're going to force a trade."

The correct frame: **does the setup exist?** If yes, take it. If no, done. The dollar outcome is irrelevant to the decision.

---

## How to Evaluate Performance

- Do not judge by individual days
- Check P&L on a **monthly** basis

> "If you wait and be patient and don't think about the money and check your P&L calendar in a month, you're going to be up. You're going to be green on the month. That's really what matters. It's not about today. It's about overall."

---

## Red Days Are Acceptable

> "You need to be okay with having a red day. It's okay to lose. You're not going to have a 100% win rate in a market that 99% of people can't beat. If you have a 60% win rate, you're still profitable."

A red day is not a failure of the strategy. It is a statistical occurrence within a positive expectancy system. The failure is responding to a red day by continuing to trade.
