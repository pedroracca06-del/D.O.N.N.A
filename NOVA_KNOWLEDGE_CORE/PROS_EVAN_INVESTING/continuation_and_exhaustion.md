## Continuation Sequence

The core continuation sequence described across all examples in this transcript:

```
1. Manipulation leg (expansion) establishes direction and range
2. Price pulls back into OTE (0.618–0.705 fib of the manipulation leg)
3. Reaction at OTE confirms the pullback is defended
4. Continuation entry taken at OTE with confluence
5. Price targets the draw (IB high/low, PDL/PDH, equal highs/lows)
```

This sequence is consistent across NY AM and Asia session examples.

## Exhaustion / Suboptimal Retracement

Exhaustion in this context = price retracing too shallow or failing to reach the OTE zone, indicating the move may not have enough energy for a clean continuation.

| Condition | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Retracement only reaches 0.5 fib | Suboptimal — likely break even or loss if entered |
| No clean reaction at OTE | Do not force an entry |
| Choppy overlapping price action in the range | No clear manipulation leg — avoid |

> "We only tap it at 0.5. So if you did take that, you probably would have got break even or took a loss on that."

**NOTE:** This transcript does not define explicit exhaustion levels beyond the 0.5 fib observation. Detailed standard deviation stretch logic is referenced but **not explained** in this video.

> "For me, I just use OTE and standard deviation and stuff like that." — Standard deviation is mentioned as part of the system but is not broken down in this transcript. Requires additional source material.

## Continuation Quality Indicators (from context)

**Higher quality continuation:**
- Manipulation leg is clear, impulsive, and measurable
- OTE zone has multiple stacked confluence factors (TDO, VWAP, fib levels)
- Internal structure swept cleanly into OTE
- Session is within the primary window (NY AM preferred)
- Directional bias is aligned with higher timeframe trend

**Lower quality / avoid:**
- Only 0.5 fib reached — no OTE tap
- Small IB range with no clear manipulation leg
- Choppy session with overlapping price action
- No confluence at OTE level

**DISCRETIONARY:** Continuation quality grading is not formalized in this transcript. The distinctions above are inferred from the examples given. Evan does not define a numeric quality score.

## Structure Break as Continuation Confirmation

Once a structural level breaks in the trade's direction, continuation is considered confirmed:

- Stop is immediately trailed to the swept internal high/low (near-zero risk)
- Trade is held toward the draw with high confidence

> "What happens when we broke this low right here? Boom. My stops were immediately put to this high right here, which was literally 1.25 points."

This is a post-entry risk management concept, not a pre-entry signal.

## Holding Trades

- Slow days: continuation can take hours to reach the draw (example: 1:20 PM hit on a slow day)
- Evan's preference: hold to the primary draw, but discretion on whether to extend to a further draw (e.g., PDL)

> "If you don't mind holding trades, if you don't mind waiting, it's perfect for you."

**DISCRETIONARY:** Decision to hold to IB level vs. extend to PDL/PDH is explicitly framed as a personal comfort judgment, not a rule.
