## Pre-Market Context

Pre-market price action is used to establish bias before the IB window:

- Pre-market sweeps of lows (bullish setup) or highs (bearish setup) are referenced as bias context
- A pre-market sweep followed by a strong open move in the opposite direction is a bullish/bearish signal

> "We come down pre-market, we sweep these lows, and then as soon as market opens, we rip to the upside."

**DISCRETIONARY:** Pre-market context is used as background bias, not as a trigger. No explicit pre-market entry rules are defined.

## Session Trade Timing

- The manipulation leg and OTE entry are typically sought **within or shortly after the IB window**
- Slow days: the draw (IB high/low) may not be reached until early afternoon (example: 1:20 PM)
- Evan holds through slow delivery if the draw and entry were correct

> "It's a very slow day that day, but we end up hitting at around 1:20 p.m. So if you don't mind holding trades, if you don't mind waiting, it's perfect for you."
