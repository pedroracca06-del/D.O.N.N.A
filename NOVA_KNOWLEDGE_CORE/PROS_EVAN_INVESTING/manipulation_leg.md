# Manipulation Leg — Definition and Identification

## Definition

> "Manipulation leg: the leg of price that pushes us in the desired direction."

The manipulation leg is the **expansion / displacement move** that:
1. Establishes the directional bias for the session or setup
2. Creates the range over which the fib is drawn
3. Defines the OTE zone where the pullback entry will be sought

In PROS terms: the manipulation leg = the **expansion** + **displacement** phase.

## How to Identify the Manipulation Leg

The manipulation leg is the **most recent impulsive move** in the direction of bias before the pullback.

**Characteristics:**
- Impulsive, directional — not choppy or overlapping
- Creates a clear high and low to anchor the fib
- Follows or establishes the session directional bias
- May sweep prior highs/lows (liquidity grab) before completing

**How to draw the fib on it:**
- SHORT: from the **high** of the leg to the **low** of the leg
- LONG: from the **low** of the leg to the **high** of the leg

> "We mark out the top, the bottom. Boom. We look for a retrace and then we look for expansion lower."

---

## What Is NOT a Manipulation Leg

| Condition | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Choppy, overlapping price action | No clean high/low to anchor the fib |
| Very small range | Insufficient range for a meaningful OTE zone |
| Multiple conflicting legs | Bias is unclear — wait for resolution |

> "I wouldn't really take anything here because it's such a small range."

---

## Manipulation Leg and Liquidity

The manipulation leg frequently **sweeps prior liquidity** (equal highs, equal lows, PDH, PDL) before creating the measurable leg. This sweep is part of the manipulation mechanism — it stops out positions in the opposite direction and generates the momentum for the continuation.

> "What does manipulation do? Stops out the opposite direction."

The sweep itself is not the entry signal. The entry comes on the **pullback into OTE after the manipulation leg completes**.
