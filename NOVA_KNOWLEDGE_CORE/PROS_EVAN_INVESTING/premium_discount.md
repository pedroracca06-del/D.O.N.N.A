# Premium / Discount Framework

**Source:** `TRANSCRIPTS_RAW/pros_fibs_002.txt`
**Extracted:** 2026-05-29

---

## Core Concept

Price does not move in one direction continuously. The market requires a **rebalancing move** (pullback) before it can generate enough liquidity to continue.

> "There's not enough liquidity generated for us to move lower. Because if a market just did this the whole day, everyone would be rich, right? There would be no retracements."

The rebalancing move delivers price into a **premium or discount zone** depending on direction — this is where the OTE entry is found.

---

## Premium vs. Discount

| Zone | Location | Who enters here |
|---|---|---|
| **Discount** | Below 0.5 of the manipulation leg range | Buyers (looking for cheap price) |
| **Premium** | Above 0.5 of the manipulation leg range | Sellers (looking for expensive price) |

> "This zone in here above 0.5 of a range is likely for sellers to step in. It just makes sense, right? They want to buy in a discounted range. Okay? Preferably 618."

**Applied to SHORT setups:**
- Manipulation leg moves DOWN
- Fib drawn HIGH → LOW
- Pullback moves price UP past 0.5 → into PREMIUM territory
- Sellers step in at 0.618–0.705 (premium) to continue the down move

**Applied to LONG setups:**
- Manipulation leg moves UP
- Fib drawn LOW → HIGH
- Pullback moves price DOWN past 0.5 → into DISCOUNT territory
- Buyers step in at 0.618–0.705 (discount) to continue the up move

The OTE zone (0.618–0.705) is always in premium for shorts and discount for longs. This is why the fib direction matters.

---

## Why the Market Retraces Into OTE

The retracement serves two mechanical purposes:

1. **Liquidity generation** — the pullback creates positions and stops that provide the fuel for the next leg
2. **Manipulation** — the retracement stops out traders who entered late on the initial move in the correct direction

> "What does manipulation do? Stops out the opposite direction."

The OTE zone is where this mechanism completes and the original directional pressure reasserts.

---

## Practical Implication

- Do not enter at 0.5 or shallower — the market has not reached the premium/discount zone yet
- 0.618–0.705 is the optimal entry because it is deep enough into premium/discount to be meaningful, but not so deep that the setup is invalidated
- 0.786 is the outer boundary of the premium/discount zone — valid but extended

**DISCRETIONARY:** The exact boundary of "too deep" is not numerically defined beyond the 0.786 outer level. Anything beyond 0.786 is described as having "very low statistics" of the original move resuming.
