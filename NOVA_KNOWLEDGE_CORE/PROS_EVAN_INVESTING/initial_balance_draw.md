## What Initial Balance Is

Initial Balance (IB) = the high and low formed during the **first hour of a session**.
It defines the session's range and serves as the **primary draw on liquidity** for the day.

IB is not an entry model. It is a **crucial context layer for every PROS setup**.

Before evaluating any PROS setup, you must first establish:
1. Where is the IB? (high, low, midpoint)
2. Which side is the draw — is price targeting the IB high or IB low?
3. Does the PROS setup direction align with that draw?

A PROS setup without IB context is unanchored — you don't know where price is going or why the continuation makes sense. IB gives the setup its destination and validates the directional bias.

## How IB Is Used

1. Mark the **IB high** and **IB low** once the window closes
2. Determine **directional bias** (bullish → targeting IB high, bearish → targeting IB low)
3. Use IB level as **TP1** — the first logical take-profit checkpoint, not a mandatory exit
4. Combine with OTE entry to structure a full trade plan

> "If you think price is going to continue higher, then you would trade up to this high. And if you think it's going to go lower, then you would trade down to the low."

---

## IB as TP1 — Not a Hard Exit

IB high/low is the **first target** (TP1), not a forced close. The decision to exit or hold at TP1 depends on volume and momentum:

| Condition at TP1 | Action |
|---|---|
| Volume weakening, momentum fading | Exit at TP1 — take the win |
| Volume still strong, price running | Hold toward 3:1 or 4:1 RR |
| Structure breaking in direction cleanly | Trail stop, let it extend |

**Do not cut a trade short just because it hit the IB level.** If the engine is still running — strong volume, momentum intact, no rejection — let it continue to a 3:1 or ideally 4:1 RR before managing out.

This is the same philosophy as ORB: TP1 is a checkpoint, not a ceiling.

---

## IB as Structural Context

The IB box (like the ORB 8:00–8:15 AM box) is a structural reference — it tells you **where price has been and where it wants to go**.

- Price consolidating within the IB = balance, no directional edge yet
- Price breaking above IB high = buyers in control, bullish draw confirmed
- Price breaking below IB low = sellers in control, bearish draw confirmed
- Price returning to IB after a break = potential retest or failed auction

Use IB to read the session's narrative, not just to mark a TP level. Before any entry, ask: what is the IB telling me about where price has already been and where it still wants to go?

## IB as Draw — Key Principles

- The IB high and low represent **resting liquidity** — price has a statistical tendency to seek one side
- Direction is determined by **bias, trend, and the manipulation leg** — IB only tells you *where* price is going, not *when* or *from where* to enter
- IB works in confluence with other draws (PDL, PDH, equal highs/lows) — the more draws stacked at the same level, the stronger the target

> "In the overall grand scheme of things, we can use this as a draw in liquidity as a super simple draw on the day."

## IB + PROS Integration

IB is the structural context that PROS operates within each session. The relationship:

| IB Role | PROS Implication |
|---|---|
| **Bias anchor** | PROS setups must align with the directional draw to IB high or low. A PROS long when the IB draw is bearish is a countertrend trade — downgrade or avoid. |
| **Target anchor** | The IB high or low is the primary TP for the PROS continuation entry. Without this anchor, there is no defined target. |
| **Manipulation leg context** | The manipulation leg in PROS ideally sweeps liquidity toward the IB and then reverses, giving the OTE entry opportunity. |
| **Quality filter** | PROS setups with IB alignment as the draw = Grade A candidate. PROS setups without a clear IB draw = Grade C or avoid. |

**Check IB first, always.** The analysis sequence for every session:
1. Lock the IB (high, low, midpoint) once the window closes
2. Determine directional draw (which side is price going to?)
3. Look for manipulation leg that sets up the OTE entry
4. Confirm PROS setup direction matches the IB draw
5. Enter at OTE, target the IB level

## IB and Choppy Sessions

- If the IB range is very small (tight consolidation), the draw is less reliable
- Evan explicitly avoids entries when the range is "such a small range" with no clear manipulation leg

> "I wouldn't really take anything here because it's such a, you know, small range."

## IB Before the Window Closes

Early entry (before 10:30 closes) is valid when:
- Bias is clearly established
- Manipulation leg is measurable
- Confluence at OTE is strong
- Price is respecting the developing range

> "Even though 10:30 did not form yet, we still have this high and bound... I can look for a long off of this level."

**DISCRETIONARY:** This is a confidence-based judgment call. No fixed rule defines when early entry is acceptable.
