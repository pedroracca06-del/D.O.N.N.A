# Behavioral No-Trade Conditions

**Source:** `TRANSCRIPTS_RAW/evan_psychology_001.txt`
**Extracted:** 2026-05-30

---

## Overview

These are internal behavioral states that should block entry even when a technical setup appears valid. A technically valid setup taken in a compromised mental state is not a high-quality execution.

---

## Hard Behavioral Blocks

The following conditions should prevent any new entries:

| Condition | Why it blocks |
|---|---|
| **Daily loss hit 2% of account** | Hard stop — session is over, no exceptions |
| **Considering Trade 2 after a loss or break-even on Trade 1** | Trade 2 is only earned by a winning Trade 1 — this is a system rule, not discretion |
| **Already took 2 trades today** | Daily limit reached — system rule, not discretion |
| **Just took a loss and have not exited the platform** | Revenge trading state — next trade is almost certainly emotional |
| **Actively trying to "make back" a loss** | The goal has shifted from following the system to recovering money |
| **No clean setup exists but a trade is being considered** | Force trade — system not aligned with charts |
| **Chasing a specific dollar target for the session** | Dollar-targeting causes forced trades when no setup exists |

---

## FOMO Block

FOMO = entering a trade because the market is moving and you feel you are missing it — not because the setup criteria are met.

> "Why do you FOMO into trades? Cuz you have a fear of missing out. That's your brain telling you you should get in because the market's dumping or the market's pumping and you want to be a part of it."

**Block rule:** If the reason for considering a trade is "it's moving and I want in" rather than "the defined setup criteria are met," do not enter.

---

## Revenge Trade Block

> "You can't go back and overtrade because you get in this mindset of, 'I lost today and I know I shouldn't have taken that trade.' You have a system. You need to act on that system when you see it. And if you don't see it, don't take the trade."

**Block rule:** Any trade taken with the explicit or implicit goal of recovering a prior loss is a revenge trade. After a loss, the session is over. The next valid entry is tomorrow.

---

## Overleverage Block

Overleveraging is listed alongside overtrading as a primary psychological trap:

> "How do you chase it? Through overtrading, through overleveraging, through not taking one trade a day."

**Block rule:** Position size must be set by the system's risk rules — not by the desire to accelerate recovery or amplify a winning streak.

---

## Social Media Contamination Block

> "A big part of why people's psychology is messed up is because of social media. You're seeing all this stuff that other people are doing. Stop comparing yourself to other people."

**Practical rule:** Decisions about whether to trade, what to trade, and how much to risk must come from the chart and the system — not from what others claimed to make on a given day. This is not a hard mechanical block, but a reminder that external social input is noise, not signal.

---

## The Spiral Pattern — Recognize and Exit

Evan describes the full failure spiral explicitly:

1. Take a forced/bad trade → lose
2. "I need to make it back" → take another bad trade
3. Lose again (or win, but rule is still broken)
4. Keep going → down half the account
5. "I already blew half, might as well blow it all" → account blown

> "Just eat the loss and walk away in the first place. It's simple."

**Recognition rule:** The moment the thought "I need to make this back" appears, the session is over. No additional entries.
